Chicken Stir-Fry with Udon Noodles

Ingredients

1 teaspoon peanut oil, plus more as needed

2 boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced

3 tablespoons soy sauce

3 scallions, white and green parts separated and sliced on the bias

2 cloves garlic, smashed and finely chopped

One 1-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and finely grated

1/2 jalapeno, seeded and cut into brunoise

2 medium carrots, julienned

4 ounces shiitake mushrooms, stemmed and sliced

3 tablespoons rice wine vinegar

1 cup chiffonade Napa cabbage

1/2 cup edamame Kosher salt

1 pound fresh udon noodles

Sesame oil, for drizzling

1/4 cup peanuts, coarsely chopped, Cilantro, for garnish


Step 1:

Coat a large, straight-sided saute pan

set over medium heat with the peanut oil

and toss in the chicken and 1 tablespoon of the soy sauce.

Cook until the chicken is cooked through,

then remove and reserve for later use.


Step 2:

Add in the white scallion pieces, garlic, ginger and jalapenos.

Add in a splash more of the oil and cook over medium heat, 1 to 2 minutes.

Toss in the carrots and cook until softened,

about 2 minutes.

Toss in the mushrooms and cook until softened,

2 to 3 minutes.

Add in the remaining 2 tablespoons soy sauce and the vinegar,

and then add the cabbage, edamame and the reserved chicken,

and cook 1 minute longer. Sprinkle in the green scallions.


Step 3:

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.

Toss in the noodles, swishing them around with a pair of tongs

so they don't stick together, and cook until soft, about 30 seconds.


Step 4:

Add the noodles to the pan,

stir to combine and cook until the noodles are hot and nicely coated.

Add a drop of sesame oil, then transfer to a serving dish and sprinkle

with the peanuts and cilantro to serve.
